Eviction and repossession

What to do if your landlord asks you to leave your home, or you are in danger of having your home repossessed.

  1. Council and housing accociation evictions

    You may be able to stop or delay the eviction.

  2. Council and housing association evictions: the court hearing

    Your council or housing association will need permission from a court to evict you.

  3. Council and housing association evictions: written notice

    Your council or housing association must give you a written warning notice that they plan to evict you.

  4. Evicting tenants (England and Wales)

    You must follow strict procedures if you want your tenants to leave your property.

  5. Evicting tenants (England and Wales): harassment and illegal evictions

    It’s a crime to harass or try to force your tenants out of a property without following correct procedures.

  6. Protection against harassment and illegal eviction: guidance

    Guidance describing tenant harassment and setting out what people can do if they are being harassed or are threatened with illegal eviction.

  7. Understanding the possession action process: guidance for landlords and tenants

    Guidance for landlords and tenants in the private and social rented sectors to explain the possession action process in the county courts

  8. Private renting for tenants: evictions

    Your landlord must follow strict procedures if they want you to leave their property, depending on the type of tenancy agreement you have and the terms of it.

  9. Private renting for tenants: evictions: accelerated possession

    Landlords can sometimes evict tenants using ‘accelerated possession’. This is quicker than a normal eviction and does not usually need a court hearing.

  10. Private renting for tenants: evictions: eviction court hearings

    If your landlord goes to court to evict you, there will be a ‘possession hearing’.

  11. Private renting for tenants: evictions: eviction notices

    If you do not leave your home by the date given in an outright possession order, your landlord can ask the court for a ‘warrant of possession’.

  12. Private renting for tenants: evictions: harassment and illegal evictions

    It’s a crime for your landlord to harass you or try to force you out of a property without using proper procedures.

  13. Repossession

    You may be able to postpone or stop your home being repossessed.

  14. Repossession: appealing a judges decision

    If you think the judge made mistakes about the law or the facts of your case in the original hearing, you may be able to appeal.

  15. Repossession: before it goes to court

    [blank]

  16. Repossession: defence form

    If your lender starts a repossession action against you, the court will send you a blank defence form and guidance on how to fill it in.

  17. Repossession: delaying eviction

    You can ask a judge to ‘suspend the warrant for possession’.

  18. Repossession: Help with legal costs

    If you’re on a low income you may be able to get legal aid.

  19. Repossession: If your home is repossessed

    Your local council must give you advice to help you find a new home.

  20. Repossession: Repossession orders

    A lender can only repossess your home if the court grants permission.

  21. Repossession: The hearing

    Repossession hearings normally take place in a judge’s chambers, not in a court room (although it’s treated as a court).
