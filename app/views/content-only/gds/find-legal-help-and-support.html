Find information about your problem

Not all problems have to end up in court. When you start dealing with a problem early, you could resolve it more easily and cheaply than going to court.

Start by doing your own research and looking at all the options available to you. There is a lot of useful information and guidance available online that can help you solve problems early.

Common legal problems

A great place to start is finding general information about your problem area to help you decide if you need to hire a legal professional or not.

You might only need some support from an organisation that knows a lot about your problem.

There are many common legal problems that can often be resolved more easily than you might expect.

Explore your problem area to find out more about your rights and how to resolve it:

Housing and homelessnes

This section covers:

  • estate agent and landlord disputes
  • estate agent or landlord refusing to carry out proper repairs to someone’s home
  • eviction notices or possession proceedings have started in court, or bailiffs are coming to evict someone
  • homelessness - person is entitled to provision by a council
  • home in disrepair and is affecting health
  • ongoing problems paying rent or rent arrears are increasing
  • someone is at risk of losing their home

Benefits

This section covers:

  • challenging a benefit decision or appealing a sanction

Debt

This section covers:

  • challenging and appealing against debts
  • receiving demands for payments by letter, phone or in person
  • receiving notices from the court about money you owe and the action the companies are taking

Employment

This section covers:

  • contract and pension disputes
  • reasonable adjustments for disabilities
  • unfair dismissal or suspension
  • unfair hiring practices
  • unfair treatment at work like discrimination, harassment or unequal pay
  • working conditions
  • workers’ rights not respected

We’ll be adding more problem types here in the near future. You can find out more about other types of problems on GOV.UK.

  • Advicenow - provides accurate, practical information on rights and the law in England and Wales
  • Citizens Advice - offers free, confidential advice online, by phone and in person
  • Legal Choices - provides independent information about legal issues and legal advisers
  • Turn2us - providing practical help to people who are struggling financially
