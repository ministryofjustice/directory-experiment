Find free and affordable legal advice and support

You can get legal advice and support from many different government departments, charities and other organisations.

^This information only applies to England and Wales.

  • Get help with a legal problem in Scotland .
  • Find free legal advice and assistance in Northern Ireland .

Legal Aid

Legal aid can help you pay for the costs of legal advice, family mediation and representation in a court or tribunal.

You’ll usually need to show that:

  • your case is eligible for legal aid
  • the problem is serious
  • you cannot afford to pay for legal costs

Check if you could get Legal Aid and where you can get it.

Exceptional Case Funding

You may be able to get Exceptional Case Funding (ECF) if your EU rights or your human rights under the European Convention of Human Rights (ECHR) are going to be breached.

You will need to show that you cannot pay for legal costs. Find out how to apply for exceptional case funding

If you’re not eligible for Legal Aid

If you’re not eligible for legal aid, a solicitor may be able to help you for free (also known as ‘pro bono’ work).

You can search for pro bono lawyers and law clinics near you on the Law Works website. Law Clinics usually offer free initial advice

If you’re on a low income you can ask questions and get free legal answers from volunteer solicitors.

If you’re on a low income you can ask questions and get Search for local sources of free or reduced-cost support on the Advice Local website.

Solicitors

You can get advice from a solicitor. Their support is not usually free.

A solicitor can:

  • give legal advice
  • translate a client's issues into legal terms
  • write legal documents like wills
  • research and prepare cases
  • help to resolve disputes and disagreements
  • liaise with other legal professionals like barristers

Find a solicitor through:

  • The Law Society if you live in England or Wales or
  • The Law Society of Scotland or
  • The Law Society of Northern Ireland

Barristers

Barristers are usually hired by solicitors to represent their clients if a case goes to court. You may be able to see a barrister without seeing a solicitor first if you live in England or Wales.

Find a barrister (known as an ‘advocate’ in Scotland) in your area through:

  • The Bar Council if you live in England or Wales
  • The Faculty of Advocates if you live in Scotland
  • The Bar of Northern Ireland
