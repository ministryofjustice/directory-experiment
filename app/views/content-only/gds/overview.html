Overview

Getting legal advice and support is often the first step to solving common problems. For example:

  • debts
  • decisions about benefits
  • employers discrimination
  • immigration status
  • disagreement with neighbours

Start by searching for trusted information and legal advice. It’s important to do this early so that you can take any necessary action and problems don’t get worse.

You may need to speak to a legal adviser with specialist training in the area of your problem. They could help you solve it, or give you advice about what to do next. This could include working with a mediator or taking other steps to solve things without going to court.

If a problem does need to be addressed in court, you can find out how to prepare for the court hearing.
