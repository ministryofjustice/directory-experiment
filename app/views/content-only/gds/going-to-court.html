Going to court

You may need to go to court to solve some legal problems. You have the right to speak for yourself in court without a solicitor or other legal professional.

If you choose to speak for yourself, it’s important to prepare for the court hearing. Find out how to represent yourself in court

If you want legal support with your court case but can’t afford to pay for it, you may be able to get help from Advocate, an organisation that provides free legal help from barristers.

Support Through Court (STC) and RCJ Advice provide free legal advice to people with a court case in civil and family courts.
