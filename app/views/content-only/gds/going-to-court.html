Going to court

You may need to go to court if you have not been able to resolve your problem by other means.

Here are some guides about the different UK courts and what to expect when you need to go to one of them:

  • Learn about the different UK courts
  • Going to civil court
  • Going to criminal court

Representing yourself in court

You have the right to speak for yourself in court without a solicitor or other legal professional.

If you choose to do this, you are known as a ‘litigant in person’ and should prepare thoroughly for your court appearance, to ensure the best result possible.

Learn about representing yourself in court (GOV.UK)

You may be able to get other legal support if you need advice about civil and family matters.

  • Advicenow helps you deal with a range of legal problems and has useful guides for people representing themselves in a court or tribunal
  • Advocate looks for free legal help from barristers for people who cannot get legal aid and cannot afford it
  • Citizens Advice offers advice about going to court without a solicitor or barrister
  • Free Legal Answershelps people on low incomes and who are not eligible for legal aid to access free, initial legal advice from pro bono solicitors
  • Legal Choicesalso offers advice on how to represent yourself in court
  • Support Through Court (STC)and RCJ Adviceprovide free legal advice to people with a court case in civil and family courts and have launched a new remote support programme, adding new ways to access their services online
