Find a legal adviser

Legal advisers can help with a wide variety of legal problems, depending on what they specialise in.

Solicitors

You can get advice from a solicitor, but their support is not always free.

A solicitor can help with:

  • giving legal advice
  • translating a client's issues into legal terms
  • writing legal documents like wills
  • researching and preparing cases
  • resolving disputes
  • liaising with other legal professionals like barristers

Find a solicitor through The Law Society if you live in England or Wales

Barristers

Barristers are usually hired by solicitors to represent a case in court and plead the case on behalf of the client and the client's solicitor.

You may be able to see a barrister without seeing a solicitor first .

Find a barrister in your area through the Bar Council if you live in England or Wales.

Free and affordable advice and support

You can get free and affordable legal advice and support from many different government departments, charities and other organisatons.

Legal Aid

Legal aid can help you pay for the costs of legal advice, family mediation and representation in a court or tribunal.

You'll usually need to show that:

  • your case is eligible for legal aid
  • the problem is serious
  • you cannot afford to pay for legal costs

Check if you qualify for Legal Aid

Exceptional Case Funding

You may also qualify for Exceptional Case Funding (ECF) if your case does not fall in scope for legal aid.

You must be financially eligible for legal aid and your case must meet the ECF criteria to qualify.

Other advice and support

There are other options where you may get support at a reduced cost or even for free. These can include law centres, law clinics, Citizens Advice and other third sector organisations.

Here are useful directory services that can help you find independent legal advisers:

  • Advice Local - a directory of independent advice organisations in your area that provide help with a variety of legal problems, like asylum and immigration, debt and money advice, disability and social care, employment and work issues, housing and homelessness, welfare benefits and tax credits
  • LawWorks - a directory of law clinics that provide free initial advice on various legal areas, such as employment, housing, consumer disputes, debt and welfare rights
