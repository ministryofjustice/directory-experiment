Find legal advice and support

You may need legal advice to resolve your problem. You can get advice from a solicitor but their support is not always free.

You could save yourself a lot of time and money by doing your own research and looking at other options available to you.

Learn about the justice system in the UK

Get advice early

If you start dealing with your problem early, you may be able to resolve it more cheaply and easily than going to court. The longer you leave it, the worse it may become and it could end up costing you much more.

There are many different kinds of legal problems and they all need to be handled in unique ways.

Free advice and support

You can get free legal advice and support from many different government departments, charities and professional advisors. Getting free help usually depends on:

  • the problem type
  • how far it has progressed
  • your financial situation

Find a legal advisor

Legal advisors can help with a wide variety of legal problems, depending on what they specialise in.

Legal advisor or solicitor

A solicitor can help with:

  1. Contentious legal work – resolving disputes between two or more parties, usually in a court or tribunal.
  2. Non-contentious legal work – dealing with the legal aspects of a client's business or personal matter, for example managing a company merger, or making a will.

Find a solicitor through:

  • The Law Society if you live in England or Wales
  • The Law Society of Scotland
  • The Law Society of Northern Ireland

Barristers

A barrister can help you with many legal issues, for example, by providing advice on your legal rights, drafting legal documents for you and representing you in a court or tribunal.

Find a barrister (known as an ‘advocate’ in Scotland) in your area through:

  • The Bar Council if you live in England or Wales
  • The Faculty of Advocates if you live in Scotland
  • The Bar of Northern Ireland

You may be able to see a barrister without seeing a solicitor first if you live in England or Wales.

Resolve problems outside of court

If you have a dispute with a person or organisation, it is often possible (and even preferable) to resolve it without going to court.

Alternative dispute resolution (ADR) is the name for a range of options for resolving legal problems, often without going through the court process.

Your legal advisor could suggest ways to sort out your problem without going to court. They can give you advice on your rights and options, or to help you negotiate with the person or organisation you have a problem with.

You may need to go to court if you have not been able to resolve your problem by other means.

Representing yourself in court

You have the right to speak for yourself in court without a solicitor or other legal professional.

You should prepare thoroughly if you choose to do this, to ensure the best possible outcome.
