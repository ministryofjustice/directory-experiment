Make a claim for constructive dismissal if you resign

Constructive dismissal can be difficult to prove so you may need more help and advice with this option.

Constructive dismissal can be on the grounds of bullying and harassment or a breaking of mutual trust and confidence.

Bullying and harrassment

There's an implied term in all contracts that an employer should take all reasonable steps to make sure their employees can do their job without being bullied or harassed.

Bullying is a behaviour that makes you feel intimidated or offended. Examples include:

  • Being treated unfairly
  • Being regularly undermined
  • Having malicious rumours spread about them

Breakdown of mutual trust and confidence

A tribunal is likely to view a breakdown of mutual trust as a breach of contract. The kinds of actions that could lead to this inlcude threatening behaviour.

The employer's actions don't need to be deliberate to be classed as a fundamental breach. An employment tribunal will look at whether the employer behaved in a way that way likely to seriously undermine trust and confidence.

Actions

  • Write a formal letter of grievance
  • Contact ACAS to work with your employer to solve the problem
  • Book an appointment with an adviser if you want to make an employment tribunal claim
